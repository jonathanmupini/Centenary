CENTRALIZED TOBACCO PRODUCTION FACILITIES

The programme entails TCM grouping small scale tobacco growers together in one area and upscaling their activities by giving them support throughout the tobacco production value chain in line with the aspirations of the NDS1 and the Tobacco Value Chain Transformation Plan. TCM is assisting the growers in getting financial support through loan guarantees. TCM is also assisting the growers with technical support and project command and control. This should transform them into viable and formal small to medium scale enterprises. The pilot project is being implemented in Mashonaland East province in the Centenary area at Jaure Estate.

TCM is working on an irrigation infrastructural development programme in partnership with local financial institutions. Through this initiative, Irrigation infrastructure such as pivots will be availed to tobacco farmers on lease. Farmers will also be able to purchase the equipment through loan facilities.

AFFORESTATION PROGRAMME

TCM is working with the Sustainability Afforestation Association (SAA) to establish eucalyptus plantations whose eventual use will be for curing of tobacco. TCM identifies the landholders and signs Joint Venture contract sharing 3 harvests on a 50:50 basis. On its part SAA uses its technical and managerial expertise to establish, maintain, and protect the woodlots. 150 Ha of woodlots have been established in Manicaland Province whilst 60 Ha have been established in Mashonaland East. The target for Mashonaland East for the current season is 120 Ha.

TOBACCO INPUT CREDIT SCHEME

TCM is working on recovering outstanding debtor amounts from the 2024/2025 season. TCM is working with CBZ Holdings to re-model the tobacco input credit scheme by leveraging on technological advancements to reduce the level of risks associated with such programmes.
